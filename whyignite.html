Seko's Story:
"The vision of We Ignite Change is to give kids the tools they need, not just to supplement their school work, but to find their passion. We believe that learning happens in more than just an academic context and kids need tools to explore their interests outside of a classroom. Our devices are more than just homework they, they are a tool for youth empowerment. This all began through a required community service class at my school--senior capstone. The class itself is based upon service learning and culminating in a student designed service learning project. It sounds very aspirational, but the sad reality is that many students, at the time, myself included, viewed it more like an onerous requirement rather than a way to give back, until I heard Seko’s story. I helped my friend with an interview at his service agency: Hawaii Literacy Project. Seko was an 11 year old boy who lived in Mayor Wright Housing, or as the kids there called it “Most Wanted Hood.” His community has an extremely high rate of domestic violence, thefts, and a large police presence. Seko’s mother runs a shop to support their family but she has chronic back pain and can’t afford pain medication which makes it difficult for her to move things around the shop. During the interview, Seko told us how he struggled academically because he has to spend most of his free time in his mother’s shop--doing the work she no longer can. What hit me the most was Seko’s drive for learning, despite all his hardships. Seko goes to the library during the little free time he has because he wants to further his education--not so he can drive a nice car or live in a big house, but so he can help kids in other countries who have it worse than him. If an 11 year old boy living in public housing wants to get an education so he can give back to others then there has to be a wayI could help him. If Seko cares so much about others that he would be willing to ignore his current financial status then there must be a way for us to help. I met Tae and Dillon, and later Zane, who came together to build We Ignite Change. We all have different skill sets but a common passion--to ensure that every kid has equal access to education. "
